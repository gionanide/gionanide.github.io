Emmanouil Gionanidis

PhD Candidate, University of Ioannina
Lead AI Researcher and Engineer, Ortho AI

I work on deep learning for medical image segmentation, with a focus on methods that survive the trip from a paper to a clinical product.

At Ortho AI, I led the development of the X-ray segmentation models powering an FDA 510(k)-cleared pre-operative templating system (K241696), plus the post-market monitoring around it. In parallel, my PhD at the University of Ioannina is part of the EU-funded UNiCA consortium on cancer treatment imaging.

Day-to-day stack: PyTorch, MONAI, TorchIO. Interests: uncertainty estimation, region-aware risk triage, and the messy engineering side of deploying segmentation models in real clinical environments.

Selected projects

FDA-cleared X-ray segmentation for pre-op templating

Ortho AI · 2023 to present

Lead the R&D of the segmentation models powering an automated radiological image-processing product for hip, spine, and knee templating. Cleared by the FDA under 510(k) K241696. Built the bootstrap-based statistical validation pipeline for regulatory reporting and the post-market monitoring stack for data drift and retraining triggers.

Mapping vulnerability descriptions to adversary techniques

IEEE SecDev · 2022

NLP-based tool that ingests vulnerability descriptions and predicts which MITRE ATT&CK adversary techniques they enable. Adopted by HivePro to help companies prioritize cybersecurity planning. Investigated the role of text augmentation in low-data regimes for this task.
